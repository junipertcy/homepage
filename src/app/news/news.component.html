H i there! I am a Senior Software Engineer at Global Healthcare Exchange, where I build and research new ways of using knowledge graphs and AI to solve problems in supply chains. Previously, I was a postdoc at CU Boulder, my alma mater, where I received a Ph.D. in Computer Science under the guidance of Dan Larremore and Josh Grochow. My work has previously been funded by The Kavli Foundation, via my advisors’ grants, and through teaching.

My work presently focuses on two application areas, plus a growing interest in systems research:

Graph and higher-order structures in social and biological systems present an analytical challenge due to their combinatorial nature. I use and create generative modeling, applied topology, and approximation schemes to uncover hidden patterns (e.g., clusters, preference, or backbones), and improve their computational efficiency.

Optimization with scarce data. With a priori knowledge, convex regularizations can leverage structured sparsity to simplify models. I extend this approach to metadata for more interpretable models. When experiments or function evaluations are costly, I use surrogate models and Bayesian optimization to select the parameters and quantify uncertainty.

Systems for deep learning. I’m especially interested in deep learning and their interaction with topology and geometry, as well as new compute or hardware paradigms that can train larger models faster and more energy-efficient.
